# ARRA Illustration Direction

## Visual Standard

ARRA illustrations use **Minimalist Vector Flat Illustration with Cel Shading**. Every image should feel precise, calm, and editorial: clean contour lines, large flat colour blocks, and hard-edged shadows that add depth without introducing visual noise. Generous paper-coloured negative space is part of the composition, not unused space.

The result should sit naturally inside the Nordic field-journal interface: restrained, contemporary, warm, and grounded in Northeast India.

## Non-negotiable Rules

- Use clean, precise outlines and simplified geometric forms.
- Build subjects and environments from large, flat colour blocks.
- Use hard-edged cel-shaded shadows on characters and background forms.
- Reserve generous white, chalk, or paper negative space around the focal scene.
- Human subjects must be Northeast Indian or Asian and shown only from the rear or a rear three-quarter angle. Faces must not be visible.
- Dress characters in modern, understated clothing: field jackets, overshirts, practical trousers, simple trainers, and minimal accessories.
- Keep the recurring male and female co-founder pair equal in scale and presence when they appear together.
- Ground landscapes in the hills, water, settlements, and working contexts of Manipur and Northeast India without reducing the region to decorative motifs.
- Use colour intentionally so the illustration blends with the surrounding page rather than appearing as a separate poster.

## Project Palette

| Token | Colour | Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Paper | `#F4F1EA` | Primary canvas and negative space |
| Chalk | `#FCFBF7` | Highlights and clear breathing room |
| Spruce | `#173C3A` | Clothing, line work, and primary dark forms |
| Fjord | `#708CA3` | Distant terrain, water, and secondary forms |
| Mist | `#DDE7E5` | Quiet background planes and atmospheric separation |
| Ochre | `#B27A22` | Sparse warmth, points of attention, and human detail |

Spruce and fjord should carry most of the scene. Ochre is an accent, not a dominant field. Shadows should be a darker flat step of the local colour rather than black overlays or blurred gradients.

## Composition

- Favour one clear focal action and a small number of supporting forms.
- Keep approximately one-third or more of the canvas as quiet negative space where the layout allows.
- Use asymmetric, editorial framing with strong visual balance.
- Keep horizons, architecture, and landscape geometry crisp and uncluttered.
- Leave room for nearby interface copy; do not bake headings, labels, or captions into the artwork.
- Crop characters from the rear so posture and direction carry the narrative instead of facial expression.

## Do Not Use

- Visible faces, front-facing portraits, or ambiguous ethnicity
- Photorealism, photography, 3D rendering, painterly texture, or anime styling
- Soft airbrushed shadows, glossy gradients, neon glows, or cinematic lens effects
- Text, lettering, numbers, logos, watermarks, or UI chrome inside the image
- Spy, covert-operations, military, Viking, fantasy, or superhero motifs
- Suits, stock-photo gestures, exaggerated poses, or romantic framing
- Busy scenery, decorative filler, or dark full-bleed backgrounds that remove the paper negative space

## Generation Brief Template

> Minimalist vector flat illustration with cel shading for ARRA. Clean precise lines, large flat colour blocks, hard-edged shadows, and generous paper-white negative space. Northeast Indian or Asian character(s) shown from the back only, with no visible face, wearing understated modern clothing. Use the ARRA paper, chalk, spruce, fjord, mist, and sparse ochre palette. Calm asymmetric editorial composition grounded in Manipur. No text, logos, watermarks, photorealism, soft shading, gradients, or fantasy motifs.

Each prompt should then add only the section-specific action, environment, and aspect ratio. This keeps the visual language consistent across future releases.

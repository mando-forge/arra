# ARRA Nordic Design System

ARRA should feel calm, precise, and patient. The design language should support an early-stage founder story without making the company look louder or more mature than it is.

## Principles

- Use light, natural surfaces first: warm paper, mist, muted teal, soft ochre, and deep ink.
- Prefer whitespace over visual decoration. Empty space is part of the message.
- Keep borders thin and quiet. Avoid stacked cards, nested panels, strong glows, and dense grid overlays.
- Use founder illustrations as atmospheric story moments, not dashboard screenshots or product proof.
- Use motion sparingly: one-time reveal, small CTA movement, and reduced-motion support.
- Avoid public-product signals such as metrics, pricing, social proof, dashboards, or launch language.

## Component Rules

- Section headings use small uppercase eyebrow text without icon clutter.
- Cards should feel like light paper surfaces: low contrast, no heavy shadows, no strong hover effects.
- CTAs use restrained color and simple motion. Avoid neon glow.
- Waypoints, rails, and corner marks are allowed only as faint structural cues.
- Image frames should not imitate apps, browsers, or product screens unless a real product is being shown.

## Copy Rules

- Speak in measured language: exploring, learning, shaping, preparing.
- Do not claim launched products, pilots, users, revenue, enterprise readiness, or proven outcomes.
- Keep ARRA's two-founder journey visible, but humble.
